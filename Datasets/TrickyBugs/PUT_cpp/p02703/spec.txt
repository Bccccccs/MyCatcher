Problem Description

You are given a graph with n cities and m bidirectional roads.
To travel along a road, you must spend some amount of money and some amount of time.

Initially, you start at city 1 with s coins.
At each city i, you may exchange time for coins any number of times.
Each exchange at city i increases your coins by c_i and increases your total time by d_i.

Your task is to determine the minimum total time required to reach each city from city 1.

Input Format

The first line contains three integers:

n m s

Each of the next m lines contains four integers:

u v a b

indicating a bidirectional road between cities u and v.
To use this road, you must spend a coins and b units of time.

Each of the next n lines contains two integers:

c_i d_i

meaning that at city i, one exchange operation increases your coins by c_i and increases your total time by d_i.

Constraints

2 ≤ n ≤ 50
1 ≤ m ≤ 100
0 ≤ s ≤ 10^9
1 ≤ u, v ≤ n
1 ≤ a ≤ 50
1 ≤ b ≤ 10^9
1 ≤ c_i ≤ 50
1 ≤ d_i ≤ 10^9

Output Format

For each city i from 2 to n, output a single integer on its own line representing the minimum total time required to reach city i from city 1.
